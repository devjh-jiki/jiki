# marketing-copy

Turn what was built into copy that makes the right reader act. Written for a developer-founder
who must sell their own product. Clear benefit over clever wording. No hype that the product can't back up.

## Workflow

1. **Extract the core**: from the user's description, pull out
   - who it's for (the specific reader),
   - the problem they feel today,
   - what the product does,
   - the one benefit that matters most.
   If the single most important benefit is genuinely ambiguous, ask one quick question before writing — marketing the wrong benefit is the most common failure. But don't stall on minor gaps: if you can make a reasonable call, state your assumption about the core benefit, write the copy, and let the user correct it.
2. **Pick the format** (or ask): landing hero, feature section, launch post (Product Hunt / 디스콰이엇), SNS/thread, release note, one-line value prop.
3. **Draft in the reader's language**: lead with the benefit, not the mechanism. Concrete over abstract. Show, don't claim.
4. **Tighten**: cut filler. Every sentence earns its place. Read it aloud; if it sounds like a brochure, rewrite.
5. **Offer variants**: give 2-3 angles (e.g. pain-led, outcome-led, curiosity-led) so the user can pick.

## Principles

- **Benefit first, feature second.** "5분 만에 배포" beats "CI 파이프라인 자동화".
- **Specific beats vague.** Numbers, names, concrete outcomes. "더 빠르게" is noise.
- **One message per piece.** If everything is important, nothing is.
- **Honest.** No claim the product can't deliver. Overpromising kills retention and trust.
- **Match the channel.** A landing hero, a thread, and a release note have different lengths and tones.
- **Korean-native.** Natural Korean, not translated-sounding English. Keep product/tech names in original form.

## Format cheatsheet

- **Hero**: one headline (benefit), one subline (how), one CTA. Under 3 lines.
- **Feature section**: 제목(benefit) + 1-2문장 설명 + 가능하면 구체적 예시/숫자.
- **Launch post**: 제목(the single highest-leverage element on a launch board — lead with the benefit or a concrete hook, not the product name alone) → 후킹 첫 줄 → 문제 → 해결(제품) → 차별점 → CTA/링크. 진정성 있는 1인칭 가능. CTA should match the launch stage (대기자/베타/정식) and invite feedback.
- **SNS/thread**: 첫 트윗이 전부를 판다. 스크롤을 멈추게. 이후는 근거/스토리.
- **One-liner**: "[누구]를 위한 [무엇], [핵심 베네핏]." 한 문장.

## Anti-patterns to avoid

- 클릭베이트, 과장된 형용사 도배("혁신적인", "최고의")
- 제품이 못 하는 것을 암시
- 기능 나열만 하고 베네핏이 없음
- 모두에게 말하려다 아무에게도 안 닿음

## When the user asks for hype

Sometimes the user will explicitly ask you to exaggerate, drop in banned superlatives, or claim something the product can't do ("바이럴 터지게 과장 좀"). Don't just comply, and don't just refuse either. Briefly explain the cost — overpromising kills retention and trust the moment the first user tries it — then reframe: virality comes from relatable specificity, not fabricated superlatives. Deliver copy that hits the user's real goal (attention, signups) honestly, and turn an early/limited product into a trust asset ("초기 버전이라 이것만은 제대로 한다") rather than hiding it.

**Regulated claims (medical, financial, health, safety, legal):** here over-claiming isn't just ineffective, it's a legal risk and can mislead people about their wellbeing. Never state or imply diagnosis, cure, guaranteed returns, or similar. Position the product as what it actually is (e.g. "기록·인지 도구", not "진단·치료"), and say so explicitly in the copy when there's any risk of misreading.
