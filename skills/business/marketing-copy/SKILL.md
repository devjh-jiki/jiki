# marketing-copy

Turn what was built into copy that makes the right reader act. Written for a developer-founder
who must sell their own product. Clear benefit over clever wording. No hype that the product can't back up.

## Workflow

1. **Extract the core**: from the user's description, pull out
   - who it's for (the specific reader),
   - the problem they feel today,
   - what the product does,
   - the one benefit that matters most.
   If any is unclear, ask before writing. Marketing the wrong benefit is the most common failure.
2. **Pick the format** (or ask): landing hero, feature section, launch post (Product Hunt / 디스콰이엇), SNS/thread, release note, one-line value prop.
3. **Draft in the reader's language**: lead with the benefit, not the mechanism. Concrete over abstract. Show, don't claim.
4. **Tighten**: cut filler. Every sentence earns its place. Read it aloud; if it sounds like a brochure, rewrite.
5. **Offer variants**: give 2-3 angles (e.g. pain-led, outcome-led, curiosity-led) so the user can pick.

## Principles

- **Benefit first, feature second.** "5분 만에 배포" beats "CI 파이프라인 자동화".
- **Specific beats vague.** Numbers, names, concrete outcomes. "더 빠르게" is noise.
- **One message per piece.** If everything is important, nothing is.
- **Honest.** No claim the product can't deliver. Overpromising kills retention and trust.
- **Match the channel.** A landing hero, a thread, and a release note have different lengths and tones.
- **Korean-native.** Natural Korean, not translated-sounding English. Keep product/tech names in original form.

## Format cheatsheet

- **Hero**: one headline (benefit), one subline (how), one CTA. Under 3 lines.
- **Feature section**: 제목(benefit) + 1-2문장 설명 + 가능하면 구체적 예시/숫자.
- **Launch post**: 후킹 첫 줄 → 문제 → 해결(제품) → 차별점 → CTA/링크. 진정성 있는 1인칭 가능.
- **SNS/thread**: 첫 트윗이 전부를 판다. 스크롤을 멈추게. 이후는 근거/스토리.
- **One-liner**: "[누구]를 위한 [무엇], [핵심 베네핏]." 한 문장.

## Anti-patterns to avoid

- 클릭베이트, 과장된 형용사 도배("혁신적인", "최고의")
- 제품이 못 하는 것을 암시
- 기능 나열만 하고 베네핏이 없음
- 모두에게 말하려다 아무에게도 안 닿음
